BRD summary stats

After a few weeks spent harvesting and integrating book reviews, it is time to share some statistics.

Over 22 millions reviews have been harvested on a sample of roughly 300K books (I use the term Book here instead of Work). I've started harvesting book sequentially (by id) and later processed them by popularity as a way to get more reviews. Some Book catalogued in Librarything could not be found in other sites while others had no reviews.

Appreciation differences and correlation

Whose site reviewers give more favorable critic? To answer that we need to compare average rating at book level.

For simplicity, let's define the metric score as being the average rating for a book on a given site (after rating was standardized on a 10 points scale). The following table gives the average and standard deviation of scorelt (Librarything), scoregr (Goodreads) and scoreba (Babelio) calculated over all books:

Site Avg Std dev
scorelt 7.496 1.639
scoregr 7.508 1.880
scoreba 6.987 2.386

Reviewers from both Goodreads and Librarything are very similar in this respect, whereas reviewers from Babelio are more harsh...before drawing conclusion, let's note that the confidence level of Babelio's average is lower (based on a smaller number of reviews as shown by its higher standard deviation).

Also, there is not much variation in user's rating, with standard deviation less than 2 on a 10 point scale, it seems most people give similar rating.

Are reviewer's between different site in agreement with each other? This can be answered by calculating correlation coefficient between the score variables:

Site Librarything Goodreads Babelio
Librarything 1 +0.377 +0.145
Goodreads -- 1 +0.18
Babelio -- -- 1

Yes all scores are positively correlated but not by much. It seems reviewers give on average similar rating, but only agree moderately on which books are good or bad. This is especially obvious between Babelio and other sites, where cultural differences seem to be at play.

Duplicated Reviews

Over 581K reviews were identified as very similar! These were found using the following rules:

  • Only comparing reviews from same Work (logical)
  • Comparing reviews with text of at least 100 characters long (ignoring similar short reviews)
  • Comparing reviews with text of similar size (±8%)
  • Flag reviews as duplicates when their trigram similarity index is above 0.7 (although this may seem small, most similar reviews had index larger than 0.9)
Metric Value
Total nb of duplicates* 581K
Avg nb of reviews per Book 110.1
Avg nb of duplicates per Book 2.89
Ratio avg duplicates/avg reviews 2.6%

* When 2 reviews are considered similar, nb of duplicates count is 2.

These numbers are important and encourage further investigation. So let's drill-down the analysis a bit further...
